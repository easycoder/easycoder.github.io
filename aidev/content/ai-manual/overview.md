# Building Smartphone-First Webapps with AI

A practical manual for teams that want fast delivery without losing clarity, reliability, or control.

## Who this is for

- Product teams building smartphone-first webapps.
- Developers and technical founders adopting AI-assisted delivery.
- Leaders who want repeatable process, not prompt roulette.

## What the manual covers (Chapters 1–8)

### 1. AI-Native Manifesto
Why constrained, readable systems can outperform heavyweight stacks for focused product delivery.

### 2. Decision Framework
A balanced case for and against high-level, human-friendly scripting languages in AI-based development.

### 3. Delivery Workflow
How to turn a blank workspace into a working MVP with disciplined prompts, guardrails, and review loops.

### 4. Troubleshooting Playbook
Root-cause debugging loop for stale state, duplicates, race conditions, and startup mismatch.

### 5. Production Habits
Release checks, observability, change control, and maintenance cadence post-MVP.

### 6. Team Playbook
Roles, handoff sequence, escalation triggers, and decision rules for small teams.

### 7. Prompt Engineering
Reusable prompt templates, anti-patterns, quality gates, and escalation prompts.

### 8. Compare and Contrast
Structured AI development versus vibe coding: trade-offs, failure modes, and where each approach fits.

## One-Page Operating Model

- Start with a human-facing entry page and an agent-facing primer markdown.
- Ship in small steps, with one intent per change.
- Require human understanding before acceptance.
- Guard against stale responses, duplicates, and unclear state transitions.
- Use explicit roles, handoffs, and escalation rules.
- Mature beyond MVP with release discipline and observability.
- Standardize prompting as a team delivery discipline.
